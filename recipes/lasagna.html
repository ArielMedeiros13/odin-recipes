Lasagna al Garfield

Description

Classic Italian lasagna made by a fat cat with layers of pasta, rich meat sauce (no joke), creamy béchamel (cause we ain't cheap, bae), and melted cheese (to please the american taste). Perfect for family dinners or when you want Garfield-level happiness.

This version is simple, beginner-friendly, and ideal for cats (to cook, not to eat). No culinary trauma required.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets, not so fluffy, not so hard
  • 500g ground beef, the good one, don't go all cheapskater on this
  • 1 onion (chopped, like, really chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (unless you have draculositis)
  • Tomato sauce, don't mess with other sauces, I'm serious
  • Salt and pepper - me likey
  • Mozzarella cheese (the foundation)
  • Parmesan cheese (or any other kind of cheese, to be honest)
  • Butter (just a tiny bit, i swear)
  • Milk
  • Flour

Steps

  1. Cook the ground beef with onion and garlic. Hear the "zzzz" sound.... so good.
  2. Add tomato sauce, salt, and pepper. Now you are cooking, big boy. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Prepare a simple béchamel using butter, flour, and milk. Here you gonna be cool as a gool.
  4. Layer pasta, meat sauce, béchamel, and cheese in a baking dish. Follow the order, don't go crazy.
  5. Repeat layers until full. Like, really full. Over the board.
  6. Finish with cheese on top. And take a lactase pill, please.
  7. Bake at 180°C for about 35–40 minutes. Or as long your heart wants to.
  8. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving. Or put it under a fan.
